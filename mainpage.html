Importance of learning a new Language

Learning a language strengthens the area of the brain which is responsible for memory, speech and sensory perception. Bilingual people are better at retaining lists, sequences, names and directions . They are also more creative, perceptive and can concentrate for longer. Learning a new language also demonstrates that you have a host of other skills. According to studies, multilinguals are better at problem-solving, more creative, and are better multitaskers. All of these skills are very attractive to any potential employer. There are financial gains to be enjoyed from learning a second language too. It‚Äôs even estimated that multilinguals earn 8% more than their monolingual counterparts.Perhaps the most important benefit of learning a language is not the most well-known. According to studies in Sweden, learning a language causes areas of the brain associated with memory, namely the cerebral cortex and hippocampus, to increase in size. Like any muscle in the body, regularly exercising your brain can make it stronger and more flexible. What's more, it has been suggested that learning a second language helps prevent brain diseases like dementia and Alzheimer's, and can even have a profound effect on your ability to understand music.
